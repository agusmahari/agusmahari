## From the Stage to the Pipeline

Before I was tuning Airflow DAGs, I was on stage — as a public speaker and Master of Ceremony, trained to read a room and make a message land. That instinct didn't disappear when I moved into data engineering; it became the thing that sets my work apart.

Day to day, I design and operate the layer most people never see: CDC pipelines that keep enterprise systems in sync within minutes instead of a day, orchestration that fails loudly instead of silently, and warehouses the business actually trusts. Then I do the part a lot of engineers skip — I explain it, in language a stakeholder who's never heard of `dbt` can still act on.

Production data engineering, paired with a communicator's instinct for clarity — that's the combination I bring to a team.

🌱 **Open to:** freelance data engineering work, consulting, and full-time opportunities
